Integral Thinking

I truly believe that common sense is not common for most of us. You’ll often get the impression from my blogs that I see most people as zombies..that is because I really do. People is mostly reactive to ideas, opinions, sounds, feelings, etc. I was like this before as well. Rarely that I find people that thinks first how a certain topic originated first before making a response.

I’ll try to put each of these significant events that I have encountered in groups in order to map where in our human capacity we acquire a certain trait or physical attribute - forcing our adaptation in the process. Post-modern category is a little bit tricky because we still don’t know much of how most of these concepts will affect our evolution as a specie in the future.

Primal:

  • Dominance Hiearrchy
  • Violence
  • Sexual Reproduction
  • Use of primitive tools - rocks, sticks

Post-primal:

  • Walking with two feet, standing straight
  • Curiosity, Art
  • Early communication through sounds and gestures
  • Use of basic tools - sharp rocks, clubs

Tribal:

  • Discovery of fire
  • Organized hunting and foraging
  • Cooking
  • Wood crafting - spears, bow and arrow
  • Trade / Barter
  • Early Religion and Supernatural beliefs

Modern:

  • Agriculture
  • Modern Religious beliefs
  • Money
  • War
  • Tax
  • Science
  • Mathematics
  • Rise of States
  • Marxism
  • Capitalism
  • Racism
  • Genocide
  • Cassic Liberalism
  • Immunization
  • Industrialization and Fossil Fuels
  • Physics and Astronomy
  • Moore’s Law
  • Pareto Principle

Post-Modern:

  • Nuclear Energy
  • Golden Rice
  • Transistors
  • Birth Control
  • Extremism
  • Internet
  • Social Media
  • Radical Left and Radical Right
  • Post-Truth
  • Machine Learning and Artificial Intelligence
  • Space Race
  • Climate change
  • Renewable Energy
  • Minimalism
  • Fermi Paradox

Whatever we do, use or say in this world… has a history so intricate that everyone I believe should atleast educate themselves in order to navigate our daily struggles. I’ll keep updating this blog as I think there is much to add in this list.
